## What happened in Mobley v. Workday?

In May 2025, a US court certified a class action against a hiring tool vendor, not exclusively the employer. The claim alleged that automated hiring decisions were discriminatory and that the vendor failed to provide adequate oversight and auditability.

This case is a turning point because it shifts liability toward the provider of the AI decision-making system as well as the organisation using it.

## Why Nigerian founders should care

Nigerian HR-tech companies are building or purchasing AI-powered hiring and candidate screening tools right now. The same risks exist whether the system is deployed locally or via an international vendor.

Under the NDPA and related employment rules, automated hiring systems that affect applicants or employees may trigger obligations for:

- transparency,
- fairness,
- bias testing,
- human intervention.

## The risk of automated decisions

AI tools that rank resumes, flag candidates or recommend hiring actions can create legally significant decisions. If your system denies a candidate or flags someone for further review, it may be considered an automated decision under NDPA provisions.

The problem is that many companies treat these systems as productivity tools rather than decision-makers.

## What to audit first

1. Identify every hiring workflow that uses AI or algorithmic scoring.
2. Determine whether the output influences employment outcomes.
3. Review your data sources for bias and representation gaps.
4. Create human-in-the-loop controls for every consequential decision.

## Vendor liability and contractual protection

If you use a third-party HR AI vendor, your contracts should include:

- clear responsibility for fairness testing,
- audit rights over training data,
- indemnity for discriminatory outputs,
- guarantees for model explainability.

The Mobley case shows that simply buying a vendor product is not enough to avoid legal exposure.

## What Nigerian HR-tech teams should build

- bias review and mitigation policies,
- documentation of algorithmic processes,
- candidate appeal workflows,
- governance controls for employee data and model updates.

## Conclusion

Mobley v. Workday is a warning that AI hiring technology cannot be deployed without legal guardrails. Nigerian founders need to treat AI recruitment products as regulated systems, not optional features.
