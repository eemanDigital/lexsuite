## Why the NDPA matters for AI-powered startups

Many Nigerian founders believe privacy law is only for big banks and telecoms. In reality, any business processing personal data with AI tools now sits directly beneath the NDPA's requirements.

The NDPA 2023 introduced specific rules for automated decision-making and personal data processing. Those obligations apply when you use AI to influence customer outcomes, make hiring decisions, personalise offers or route user journeys.

## The automated decision-making trap

Section 46 of the NDPA gives individuals the right not to be subject to decisions made solely by automated processing where those decisions significantly affect them. That phrase is not academic — it applies to credit scoring, candidate screening, automated onboarding decisions, and even algorithmic employee monitoring.

A Nigerian startup using an AI model to approve loans or screen applicants may be required to:

- disclose the use of automated decisions,
- provide information about the logic used,
- preserve a human review mechanism,
- allow affected individuals to contest the results.

Most teams do not realise that an off-the-shelf model powering product flows can trigger these obligations.

## Lawful basis is not optional

The NDPA requires a lawful basis for every processing activity. In practice this means:

- consent for marketing and personalised communication,
- legitimate interests for basic analytics,
- contractual necessity when you process data to deliver a service.

When AI is involved, the exercise of judgement becomes legal as well as technical. You cannot treat an AI integration as a “product feature” and ignore the data protection risk.

## Breach notification is mandatory

The NDPA requires affected individuals and the NDPC to be notified within 72 hours of a personal data breach. If your AI platform mishandles biometric data, scraping credentials, or leaks customer insights, the clock starts immediately.

For AI-first products, the most common breaches are:

- insecure model training pipelines,
- insufficient access controls on datasets,
- vendor leaks from external AI providers.

Design your AI architecture with breach response in mind — not as an afterthought.

## Practical steps for founders

1. Map each AI use case to the NDPA's lawful basis rules.
2. Identify where automated decisions are made and add clear disclosures.
3. Review contracts with AI vendors for joint responsibility.
4. Create a DPIA for all AI systems processing personal data.
5. Build a rapid breach response plan with NDPC reporting triggers.

## What this means for you

If you are a Nigerian startup using AI tools to make customer, employee or credit decisions, you should assume exposure until proven otherwise.

The NDPA is not just a privacy law — it is a governance law. Founders who treat it as a checklist risk being outpaced by competitors who embed privacy and AI accountability into product design.
