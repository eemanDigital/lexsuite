## What the Startup Act means for founders

The Nigeria Startup Act 2022 creates an opportunity for technology entrepreneurs to access support, fast-track registration and tax relief.

If your business qualifies, you can accelerate funding readiness and secure incentives that are rarely available through standard corporate channels.

## Who can qualify?

The Act is designed for innovative startups that are:

- registered in Nigeria,
- technology-led,
- less than 5 years old,
- capable of scaling.

This includes software platforms, fintech products, AI applications, healthtech, agritech and other digital innovations.

## Benefits available

Qualified startups may receive:

- tax exemptions during the early years,
- reduced registration fees,
- access to investor networks,
- support for accessing grants and finance.

These benefits are especially valuable for founders looking to conserve cash while they build product-market fit.

## Fast-track registration process

The Startup Act provides a streamlined approval route through the relevant government agency. The process typically includes:

1. eligibility assessment,
2. business plan review,
3. applicant interview,
4. issuance of a Startup Service Provider certificate.

A legal advisor can help ensure that your pitch, valuation assumptions and corporate structure align with the eligibility criteria.

## Common mistakes founders make

- assuming they are too early-stage to qualify,
- failing to document technology or innovation clearly,
- using an incorrect company type for the application,
- neglecting NDPA and compliance requirements during the process.

The strongest applications connect product innovation to a sustainable business model.

## What to prepare

Your application should include:

- a clear description of the product or service,
- evidence of technology development,
- a scalable business plan,
- ownership and governance structure,
- compliance checks for data privacy and regulation.

In many cases, a founder-level legal review is what turns a strong concept into an approved Startup Act application.

## Why this matters now

As funding becomes more competitive, early-stage teams that can show a proven legal foundation and regulatory advantage will stand out.

The Startup Act is a business signal: it tells investors and partners that the company is operating with support from the national innovation framework.
